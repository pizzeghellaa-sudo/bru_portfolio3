## Project
Il Marmo — Editorial System for Material Culture

---

## Objective

Create a case study page that presents Il Marmo as an editorial design project focused on material, architecture, and visual storytelling.

The page must communicate a strong editorial identity, not a collection of spreads.

---

- Swiss editorial layout
- strong typography
- black & white aesthetic
- high contrast imagery
- focus on rhythm and pacing
- minimal but precise text

### CONTEXT

Il Marmo is an editorial project exploring the relationship between material, architecture, and visual culture.

The publication translates the physical qualities of marble into a structured visual narrative, balancing imagery, typography, and spatial rhythm.

### 01 — IDEATION

The project explores how material qualities can be translated into editorial form.

Marble is treated not only as a subject but as a visual language, guiding composition, contrast, and pacing throughout the publication.

Visuals:
- texture close-ups
- abstract material images

---

### 02 — STRUCTURE

A modular editorial system was developed to organize content across different types of pages.

Layouts define a balance between full-bleed imagery, structured text blocks, and mixed compositions.

Visuals:
- spreads with clear grid
- layout variations

---

### 03 — VISUAL LANGUAGE

The visual language is based on contrast between texture and space.

Black and white photography emphasizes material depth, while typography remains restrained to support clarity and rhythm.

The result is a publication that feels both tactile and architectural.

Visuals:
- strongest spreads (1 large, 2 smaller)
- texture vs architecture contrast

---

### 04 — EDITORIAL RHYTHM

The sequence of pages alternates between dense imagery and structured content, creating a controlled reading pace.

This rhythm reinforces the perception of material, allowing moments of visual immersion and informational clarity.

Visuals:
- sequence of 2–3 spreads showing variation

---

### 05 — OUTCOME

The project results in a cohesive editorial system where material, layout, and typography work together to create a consistent visual narrative.

A publication designed to communicate material culture through structure and rhythm.

---

## Image Rules

- prioritize strongest spreads

## Layout Notes

- emphasize asymmetry
- create one dominant visual section
- maintain strong spacing rhythm
